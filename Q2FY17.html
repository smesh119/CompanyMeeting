FY17 goals update

DeLisa Alexander, EVP + Chief People Officer

SCALING OUR CULTURE
  • Red Hat’s culture is a unique source of our competitive advantage, and we need to take a purposeful approach to shaping it as we grow
  • There’s been a lot of discussion on Mojo and memo-list about scaling our culture, including what we don’t want to lose and what needs to shift
SOME THEMES THAT EMERGED FROM THIS FEEDBACK
  • Continue and improve transparency, freedom, and collaboration, while building a better infrastructure of systems, tools, processes, and guidelines to support our growth
  • There’s some tension between sustaining our individual freedom (which helps us meet our business objectives quickly) and the shared cost and complexity that we all experience as a result
  • Our challenge as the open source leader is to figure out how to find the right balance as we grow
PROPOSED FOCUS AREAS (HYPOTHESES)
  • Continue reinforcing the Red Hat values and leadership behaviors (Red Hat Multiplier)
  • Find resolution for the endless debate over what tools and technology we use--develop a workable position that respects our open source roots and our business realities
  • Start creating clear, consistent processes and procedures that make our jobs easier, with enough flexibility to handle exceptions where justified and possible
  • See the full summary of feedback, data, and our hypotheses: http://red.ht/2d4DNBq

Q2 Financials

Frank Calderoni, EVP, Operations + CFO

  • Although our stock price was up 6% after hours, this was a good quarter, but not great
  • After speaking to investors, we heard consistent themes that caused them concerns:
  • Cash flow was down 21% year over year ($97M versus consensus at $127M)
  • Emerging products did not perform as expected--in particular, middleware. We were down 7% comparatively.
  • Our top renewals were down 10% of deal value versus our normal 115%. However, we did beat most of their expectations. Our total revenue was $600M and operating margin was 22.8%. We also raised our guidance slightly for the full year 2017. This is called a "beat and raise," which helped our stock price increase the following day after earnings were announced.
  • We need to pay attention to our revenue growth, which was 18%, yet our expense growth was 25%
  • We hired 1000+ associates in the first half of FY17, and although we are proud to be bringing in such great talent, we hired more Red Hatters than planned
  • In the second half of FY17 and for FY18, we’ll need to revisit our hiring plan to bring our expense growth in line with revenue growth
  • The bonus pool for Q2 was funded at $19.97M
  • This was a good quarter, and now we need to work together to continue our great performance through the second half of FY17
